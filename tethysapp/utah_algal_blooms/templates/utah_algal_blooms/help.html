Frequently Asked Questions


What are algal blooms?

Harmful algal blooms are composed of cyanobacteria or blue-green algae which form as a result of eutrophication.


Why have there been so many algal blooms in Utah recently?

Over the past several decades, higher amounts of nutrients, specifically nitrogen and phosphorus have been input into water bodies as a result of agriculture and wastewater processing. These nutrients directly contribute to eutrophication.


Can I enter historical algal bloom data in addition to present data?

Yes, you can enter data from as far back as you have records of harmful algal blooms.
